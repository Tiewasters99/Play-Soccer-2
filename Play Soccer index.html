About Our Founder

Judy McPherson

Founder & Manager

Play Soccer Trenton is led by Judy McPherson, a former international banker with a lifelong passion for community service. Following a distinguished career in finance, Judy moved to Mount Desert Island, Maine, after she and her husband retired from the Princeton, New Jersey area, where Mr. McPherson served as Superintendent of Schools.


Despite the distance, Judy remains deeply committed to Mercer County. Supported by the NJ Department of Recreation, she manages PST with a vision to expand our affiliation from 16 schools today to 19 schools by 2026.

Our Programs

Bonding Beyond Bars

Restorative Justice Focus: An innovative program allowing incarcerated fathers to spend recreational time playing soccer with their children. This eases the path for reentry and maintains critical family bonds.

Purposeful Play

We use soccer as a vehicle for education. Our curriculum integrates sport with social-emotional learning, teaching conflict resolution, leadership, and teamwork on and off the pitch.

Health & Wellness

Supported by county health initiatives, we focus on physical activity to combat sedentary lifestyles, alongside nutritional education to support healthy living for inner-city youth.

International Roots

Play Soccer Trenton was formerly part of Play Soccer Network International (PSNI), a global network spanning six African countries dedicated to using soccer for education and wellness.

The international group is headquartered in Ghana and chaired by the distinguished international businessman Kweku Awotwi. While we now operate independently in New Jersey, we carry forward the PSNI mission of community building and support.

World Cup 2026 & Future Goals

With the World Cup craze about to hit the United States, there has never been a better time to get involved. We are creating opportunities for inner-city children to engage with the sport and healthy living principles during this historic event.

Goal: Expanding to 19 Mercer County schools by the end of 2026.
